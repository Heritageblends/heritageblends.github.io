Since a grandmother's kitchen

We grew up between drying mats and the smell of roasting coriander.

Spice Shala began on a verandah in Kizhakkambalam, where three generations sorted cardamom pods by lamplight and argued over the right ratio for garam masala. We never wrote it down — we tasted until it was right.

Today we work with the same estates our family bought from, grade by hand, and mill in small lots so nothing sits losing its oils. What reaches you is what we'd cook with ourselves.

The ritual

Three steps from tin to table.

  1. 01

    Bloom

    Warm the spice in a dry pan until it speaks — thirty seconds, no more. The kitchen tells you when.

  2. 02

    Grind

    Crush fresh. Pre-ground spice is a memory of flavour; this is the flavour itself.

  3. 03

    Fold

    Add late, off the heat where you can. Heat is a thief of the top notes you paid for.
